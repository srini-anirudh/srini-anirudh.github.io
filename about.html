About Me!

I am a Senior Research Engineer at BharatGen, where I lead the Vision-Language Models team. I spend most of my time building multimodal and multilingual foundation models and agents for Indian document intelligence: models that can read, parse, reason over, ground, reconstruct, and evaluate documents across languages and domains.

Senior Research Engineer and VLM team lead at BharatGen.

Research focus: document intelligence, grounding, parsing, reconstruction, and multilingual VLMs.

IIT Hyderabad CSE graduate and Institute Gold Medalist, Rank 1 out of 1103.

Curious about Neuromorphic AI and Quantum Machine Learning.

My current work sits at the intersection of vision-language modeling, document understanding, visual grounding, layout analysis, synthetic data generation, and scalable VLM training systems. Recent projects include Patram-Layout, Patram-Grounder, Patram-Parse, Patram-Bench, Patram-Core, Patram-Ingest, and Patram-FS. My research has appeared at venues including CVPR and ICDAR.

I graduated from the Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad with a B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering. I received the Institute Gold Medal for Excellence in Academics and Co-curricular Activities, ranking 1st out of 1103 students.

Before my current role, I was a Founding ML Engineer at BharatGen, where I helped build Patram, India's first VLM for document understanding, along with Patram-Bench, Patram-DB, DarshinAI, and e-vikrAI. I have been fortunate to be mentored by Dr. Ravi Kiran Sarvadevabhatla and Dr. Ganesh Ramakrishnan.

I am also intrigued by Neuromorphic AI and Quantum Machine Learning. As an undergraduate, I explored far-field speaker recognition, quantum methods for vehicle routing, LiDAR semantic segmentation, neuro-fuzzy clustering, adversarial robustness for compiler embeddings, neuromorphic AI systems, and motion generation.

When I am not building document AI systems, I enjoy:

  • mentoring and teaching; I have served as Head TA for Data Structures and TA for Intro to Programming,
  • reviewing research on document layout analysis, document reconstruction, and VLM benchmarks,
  • following ambitious AI systems, sports, music, and the occasional side quest into random facts,
  • thinking about how Indian-language AI systems can become more useful, accessible, and grounded.
